Birds of PreyIn Canada
Bald Eagle
One of the largest Canadian birds of prey, the bald eagle is a bird of prey who can be found at the top of the food chain. Their diet mostly consists of fish (exceeding 50%), but can be found digging into mammals, birds and other prey.

Red-tailed Hawk
Beautiful from afar, the red-tailed hawk feeds more on mammals like rodents and rabbits - with the occasional snakes, birds and ducks. The red-tail betrays its identity as it can be seen gliding through the sky in search of its prey using its keen eyesight.

Great Grey Owl
Nocturnal by nature, the Great Grey Owl makes little noise as it's specialized feathers sail through the air. Hunting close to the ground, the great grey primarily hunt small rodents. Their chirping manifests in a chilling hooting noise and use their superior hearing ability to hunt.
